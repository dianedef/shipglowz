# 204-sg-market-study

> Turn a product idea or niche into a decision-ready market study with demand, competition, monetization, and a clear go/no-go signal.

## What It Does

`204-sg-market-study` researches whether a market is worth entering before you spend weeks building in the dark. It combines search demand, trend data, competitor analysis, market sizing, and monetization framing into one structured output.

For a solo founder, the value is simple: it helps you avoid building for a market that is too small, too crowded, or too vague to win.

## Who It's For

- Solo founders validating a SaaS, content site, service, or niche product
- Operators comparing multiple ideas before committing
- Builders who want evidence, not vibes, before choosing a market

## When To Use It

- when you have a product idea and need to know if demand is real
- when you want to compare a few niches before choosing one
- when you need a market brief to support `BUSINESS.md`, GTM, or pricing decisions
- when you need a market brief that should align with the portfolio project pitch links index

## What You Give It

- a niche, problem space, or product idea
- optionally, target countries or markets
- optionally, existing business context from the repo

## What You Get Back

- a structured market-study report
- demand and trend signals
- competitor and SERP observations
- a rough TAM/SAM/SOM view
- monetization and positioning recommendations
- an explicit go / no-go or conditional-go conclusion

## Typical Examples

```bash
/204-sg-market-study
/204-sg-market-study "AI bookkeeping for freelancers"
/204-sg-market-study "French speaking therapy marketplace"
```

## Limits

- It improves decision quality, but it does not guarantee product-market fit.
- It depends on available external data and can be weaker in obscure or brand-new markets.
- Market research still needs founder judgment on execution, timing, and distribution.
